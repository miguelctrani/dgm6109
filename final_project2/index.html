Adventure Experiences

Lighthouse Park

Just a 30-minute drive from downtown, Lighthouse Park in West Vancouver offers numerous easy hiking trails among some of the oldest Douglas firs in the region. Dogs are allowed off-leash throughout the park, making it a favorite spot for dog walking on the North Shore. Many of the trails follow the coastline and are mostly flat, though there are a few steeper sections. A 15-minute walk leads to Starboat Cove, a great picnic spot with views stretching from the Lions Gate Bridge to the University of British Columbia and Vancouver Island.

Stawamus Chief

Just an hour from Vancouver, Stawamus Chief offers one of the region's most classic, yet challenging, hiking trails. This 11-kilometer (6.8-mile) route covers 600 meters (1,968 feet) of elevation gain and offers sweeping views of the mountains and water. The reward for this strenuous hike is the summit, an opening in smooth rock that invites a picnic or a nap in the sun. The hiking trail that skirts the forested back of the mountain features old-growth forest and a side trail option for views of the majestic Shannon Falls.

Stanley Park

Considered one of the world's largest urban parks, Stanley Park offers hikers of all levels a variety of outdoor trails. The paved seawall features separate walking and biking lanes, allowing for a leisurely stroll along the park's waterfront. Get a workout with a 6-kilometer (3.7-mile) loop through the temperate rainforest. The trail passes through the park gardens and Beaver Lake, ending near Third Beach.

A First Look at Dine Out Vancouver 2026

Vancouver's culinary scene is a dream come true for foodies looking to expand their palates and savor flavors from around the world. And there's no better way to experience it all than through the city's numerous food festivals. With the arrival of winter comes the most anticipated of these celebrations: Dine Out Vancouver.

For the past 24 years, the preparations for Dine Out Vancouver have followed a similar pattern. Everyone counts down the days to the grand reveal of the restaurant lineup. It arrives, menus are reviewed, and wish lists are created.

Restaurant Preview in Mount Pleasant

Just a 10-minute Uber ride from downtown, you'll find yourself in Mount Pleasant, one of the city's most vibrant neighborhoods. Home to countless independent boutiques and neighborhood cafes, it's also a haven for Vancouver foodies.

At the heart of this lively neighborhood is The Acorn, one of the city's most celebrated vegan restaurants. It serves some of the most creative dishes in town, crafted with fresh, locally sourced ingredients in a zero-waste kitchen.

Your Last-Minute Summer To-Do List

Now that September is here, fall is just around the corner. But summer isn't over yet! You still have time to enjoy some last-minute adventures on the beach, hiking, visiting markets, and more.

Grouse Mountain

Grouse Mountain is open year-round, with skiing, skating, tobogganing, and much more in the winter. But you only have a few weeks left to enjoy its summer activities. Don't miss the world-famous Lumberjack Show.

Go Paddleboarding

In Vancouver, you can enjoy paddleboarding right downtown, on the beach, or along the rugged coastline. While kayaking is available year-round, most paddleboard rentals and tours wind down with the arrival of winter.

What is HYROX?

Known as the "world series of fitness racing," HYROX is an indoor fitness competition that has exploded in popularity worldwide since its inception in 2017. What makes it unique is its standardized race format with eight 1 km running laps alternating with 8 different functional workout stations.
