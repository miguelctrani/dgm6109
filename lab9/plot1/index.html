Plot 1: Study Time vs Performance - My Breakthrough Sessions

Hypothesis: As study time increases, problems solved increases (filtered to show only successful sessions where I solved 5+ problems and actually studied).

Why This is One of My Best Scatterplots

This visualization represents one of my best scatterplots because it uses Array.filter() strategically to isolate only my most successful study sessions. By filtering for sessions where studyTime > 0 AND problemsSolved >= 5, I eliminated two types of noise: days I didn't study at all, and days where I was just learning concepts without solving problems.

The filter revealed a powerful pattern: all 7 of my breakthrough sessions (solving 5+ problems) occurred between October 20 and November 5, 2025. What's remarkable is that these successful sessions show a clear correlation between high confidence (larger circles) and low frustration (green colors). The filter helped me discover that my best work happened when I was calm and confident, not when I was grinding through frustration.

The most interesting insight: study time alone doesn't predict success. I had sessions with 120 minutes (Oct 18) that only produced 4 problems solved, while sessions with 30-50 minutes in November produced 8 problems solved. The key difference? My mental state, shown through the confidence and frustration indicators.

This filtered view was genuinely useful—not just a requirement. It transformed my raw data into actionable insight: quality study sessions with the right mindset matter more than simply logging hours.
